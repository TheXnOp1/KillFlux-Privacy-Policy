KillFlux Privacy Policy

com.xn.killflux  ·  Android App

Last updated: April 21, 2026

Short version: KillFlux does not sell your data. We collect minimal analytics to improve the app. All app-killing operations happen entirely on your device.

1. Introduction

KillFlux ("we", "our", or "the app") is an Android application that helps users manage and terminate background applications using the Shizuku privileged API.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data. By using KillFlux, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

2. Information We Collect

📊 Analytics Data (Firebase Analytics)

We use Firebase Analytics to collect anonymous usage data including: app opens, feature usage frequency, crash reports, and device type/OS version. This data does not contain any personally identifiable information.

📱 Advertising Data (Google AdMob)

Free users see ads served by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect device identifiers and usage data to serve relevant ads. You can opt out of personalised ads via your device settings: Google Settings → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalisation.

💳 Purchase Data (Google Play Billing)

If you subscribe to KillFlux Pro, your purchase is processed entirely by Google Play. We receive only a confirmation token. We do not have access to your payment card details.

🔧 Shizuku / Shell Access

KillFlux uses the Shizuku API to execute shell commands (such as am force-stop) on your device. These commands run entirely locally on your device. No shell output or command history is transmitted to our servers.

3. Information We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, or any contact information
  • We do not collect the list of apps you kill or your whitelist
  • We do not collect your scheduler rules or automation settings
  • We do not store or transmit any data processed by Shizuku
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing
  • We do not access your contacts, messages, camera, or microphone

4. How We Use Your Information

The limited data we collect is used solely for:

  • App improvement — understanding which features are used most
  • Crash diagnosis — identifying and fixing bugs
  • Ad delivery — serving ads to free-tier users (via Google AdMob)
  • Subscription management — verifying Pro subscription status via Google Play

5. Third-Party Services

KillFlux uses the following third-party services, each governed by their own privacy policies:

  • Google Firebase Analytics — anonymous usage analytics
  • Google AdMob — advertising (free users only)
  • Google Play Billing — subscription processing
  • Shizuku — privileged shell API (no data collection)

6. App Permissions Explained

  • QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES — required to list installed apps for the kill list
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE — required for the Scheduler service to run in background
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS — required to show the persistent Scheduler notification
  • ACCESS_WIFI_STATE — required for Wi-Fi connected/disconnected scheduler triggers
  • INTERNET — required for Firebase Analytics and AdMob

KillFlux does not request location, camera, microphone, contacts, or storage permissions.

7. Data Storage and Security

All user preferences (whitelist, scheduler rules, settings) are stored locally on your device using Android SharedPreferences. This data never leaves your device.

We have disabled Android's backup feature (android:allowBackup="false") to prevent your app settings from being included in device backups.

8. Children's Privacy

KillFlux is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

9. Your Rights

Since we do not collect personally identifiable information, there is minimal personal data to request or delete. However, you have the right to:

  • Opt out of personalised ads via Android device settings
  • Reset your advertising ID in Google Settings
  • Uninstall the app to remove all locally stored preferences
  • Contact us with any privacy-related requests or questions

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any significant changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Questions or Concerns?

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us.
